Using Google Docs for RPs
Heya, have a question for all RPers... Have you ever used Google Docs, or any sort of real-time sharing document for writing?

If you haven't, basically you share a document together and you can write/edit/format in real-time together, and discuss while writing it.

If you have, do you find it useful? Have you discovered any pros or cons to using it?

For me, I find it really useful for one-on-one conversations between two characters, since it can cover the entire conversation without waiting for responses. Action is faster-paced, multiple characters have input in a condensed post, etc. I guess I could break it down:

Pros:
- Helps speed up conversation, especially one that is just shared between two characters.
- A lot can be finished in little time if both (or more) people are on for the post.
- Battles can be quicker, especially character vs character, and flow better.
- Dialogue can be smoother, since people are working on the overall post, and can get input from the other RPer if they want to rework a dialogue line, or change a sentence.
- If they're both not on at the same time, one can add their part, and wait for the other person to add theirs the next time they come on. That way little paragraphs can be added over time.
- An extra hand in editing/typo fixing, formatting, grammar, etc.
- It's good for 'the one epic post' where plot is revealed, or something major comes to light. People can have their characters add a little dialogue or reactions during the twist or major event, even influence a little.

Cons:
- Some RPers can feel excluded, especially people in different timezones or people who don't use docs.
- Sometimes too much progress is made, which again, someone might feel left out. Especially if they feel left out of a post they wanted to be in, and was the only one not accounted for.
- A lot of inner dialogue/thoughts/description is sacrificed purely for physical actions, and spoken dialogue. No paragraphs of a character conflicted or pondering - sometimes it's still there, but it's glossed over and focused on the interaction.
- Sometimes people get so reliant on the co-posts, they ignore solo, and feel they need to work with other people to make any progress. So when there's no co-posts being written, nothing is really done outside of it.
- Pacing is even more inconsistent, especially if you're waiting for a certain amount of people to be on at the same time. Sometimes you can encourage the 'just add a bit, and don't worry if someone isn't there', but people usually like to wait for others.

So yea, I'd like to know what others think. ^_^v

Yes. Used them and loved them.

I've performed roleplays on them as well as kept persistent world information on them. Rather handy when you can share one document of setting and character information with multiple people, and give who you want access to edit and update and add to that information. Plus, since everything's stored in a gmail account, I can access it on my laptop, desktop, or on school computers without having to transfer files, as long as I can connect to the internet. It might sound sort of like a forum, but for those of you that have never seen a googledoc, it's basically like any word document. Text can be formatted, pictures added, etc. Only, you can literally see someone else typing in them if you have the document open while they do. I'm presently using them to supplement one of my 1x1's on RPG, in fact.

Great tool for RP.
