Mina walked through the snow-bound forest, her jaws stained with the redness of blood from her last kill, and her yellow eyes gleaming in their black surrounded sockets. Her feet dug into the snow like a snow-plow, and her nostrils were moving in and out ever so slowly. The trees that surrounded the forest all looked down at her, staring into her sadenned soul. The forest would soon lead her into the Eternal Mountain, where she would choose to rest her aching bones.

Leisha heard loud barks rumbling across the mountain. Indeed, they sounded like that of a dog's, but somehow it sounded... different. It had much more ferocity than a domestic dog would have. And, of course, the only wild dogs in this area would be wolves.

And a wolf means companionship, she told herself.

Without much difficulty, she leapt towards the sound of the barking, yet still making sure she was mostly hidden by the forest, just in case. Even if the one barking was not a threat, there could be hunters just lurking around the corner. And hunters were threats. Huge threats.

There it was again. The same smell on the wind, but slightly different this time. A wolf, maybe two were coming closer, moving cautiously. Garrett gave one more sniff at the leg bone and rose. He reared back on his haunches and stuck his nose high in the air. Maybe he would be able to tell more about the wolves stalking up behind him.

Moving gently across the wind was the smell of blood. Fresh rabbit. Freshly killed rabbit. Garrett jaws open just slightly to let the thin stream of saliva drip down. He was hungry and cold. Maybe someone would have a meal he could share. Hopefully he could find the rabbit before it was all gone. Or, if his nose was correct, that storm came and broke against the mountain.
